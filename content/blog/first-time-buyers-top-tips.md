I'm often asked for my top tips for first-time buyers, so here they are. Three things I'd recommend doing before you even start looking at properties.

## 1. Get an Up-to-Date Credit Report

There are two good reasons for this.

First, it could remind you about credit you'd forgotten all about. An old phone contract, a store card you haven't used in years, a student overdraft still technically open. These all count, and you'll need to include them in your affordability assessment.

Second, it could flag up anything you'd want to resolve on your credit report before making a mortgage application. A missed payment you didn't know about, an incorrect address, or a default that's nearly dropped off. These are things you want to deal with in advance, not discover when the lender runs their checks.

There are free services that let you check your credit report without affecting your score. It takes five minutes and could save you weeks of delays.

## 2. Know Your Deposit

It's really important that you know what your deposit is, or the deposit you're working towards with your savings.

This matters because different lenders will lend you different amounts depending on what your deposit size actually is. A 5% deposit opens certain doors, 10% opens more, and 15% or above gives you access to the most competitive rates.

Knowing your deposit figure means we can give you accurate advice from day one, rather than working with estimates that might not hold up when it comes to the application.

## 3. Understand That It's About Affordability

Residential mortgages these days are based on affordability, not just a simple income multiple. Lenders look at your income, your regular outgoings, your existing credit commitments, and your lifestyle costs to work out what they're comfortable lending you.

Two people earning the same salary can be offered very different amounts depending on their outgoings. Understanding this upfront helps set realistic expectations about what you can borrow and what properties are within reach.
