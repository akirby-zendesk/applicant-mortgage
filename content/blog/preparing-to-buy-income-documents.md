Welcome to day three of my five-day guide to preparing to buy a new home. Yesterday we covered [checking your credit history](/blog/preparing-to-buy-credit-history). Today it's all about income, and the documents I'd be looking for.

The documents you need depend on how you earn your income. Here's a breakdown by employment type.

## If You're Employed

I'd be looking for your **latest three months of monthly payslips** as a starting point. However, if you're on commission, receive a monthly bonus, or work under the CIS scheme, lenders may look for more than this, though three months is still a good start.

If you receive an **annual bonus**, then the last two years of corresponding bonus payslips would also be required.

Try to dig out your **latest two years of P60s** as well, since these confirm your total annual earnings and tax paid, and most lenders will want to see them.

### Starting a New Job?

If you have a new role lined up, your **new contract with your agreed start date** would also be necessary. Many lenders will accept this as part of the application, so a job change doesn't have to hold things up.

### On Maternity Leave?

If you're on maternity leave, please evidence:
- The **pay you were receiving before you left**
- Your **latest three months of maternity payslips**
- Your **return-to-work letter** confirming your future income and return date

This allows lenders to assess your income based on what you'll be earning when you go back, not just what you're receiving now.

## If You're Self-Employed

We'll be looking for your **latest three years of tax calculations and tax year overviews** (SA302s). These are the core documents that tell lenders what you've been earning.

## If You're a Limited Company Director

In addition to your tax documents, you'll need your **latest three years of full company accounts**. Don't worry if your company hasn't been trading quite that long, because there are lenders that will work with two years of accounts, and some (though fewer) that would even consider one year.

## If You're a Contractor

Please evidence your **latest two years of contract history**, as well as any ongoing or upcoming contracts you have. Lenders who specialise in contractor mortgages will want to see continuity of work and your current day rate.

## If You're a Landlord

You'll also need to **evidence your buy-to-let income**: rental statements, tenancy agreements, or your tax return showing rental profits.

## The Key Takeaway

The more organised you are with your documents upfront, the smoother the application process will be. Gathering these now means we can move quickly when the time comes.
