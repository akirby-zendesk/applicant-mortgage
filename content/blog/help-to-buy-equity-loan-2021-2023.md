The Help to Buy equity loan scheme ran from 2021 to March 2023, helping first-time buyers get onto the property ladder with a government-backed loan. While the scheme has now closed to new applicants, it's worth understanding how it worked, especially if you're still in the early years of a Help to Buy mortgage and need to plan ahead.

Here are the five key things to know.

## 1. You Had to Be a First-Time Buyer

The scheme was exclusively for people buying their first home. If you'd previously owned a property, whether in the UK or abroad, you weren't eligible.

## 2. The Loan Was Between 5% and 20% (40% in London)

The government equity loan could cover anywhere between 5% and 20% of the property purchase price. If you were buying in London, this went up to as much as 40%, reflecting the higher property prices in the capital.

Combined with your own deposit (minimum 5%) and a mortgage for the remainder, this meant you could buy with a relatively small amount of savings.

## 3. The Property Had to Be a New Build

Only newly built homes qualified for the scheme. This was designed to stimulate new housing supply as well as help buyers. Existing properties, however good the deal, weren't eligible.

## 4. Maximum Purchase Prices Varied by Region

The scheme introduced regional price caps. The maximum purchase price depended on where the property was located. For example, in the South East the cap was £437,600, while other regions had lower limits. This was a change from the earlier version of the scheme, which had a flat £600,000 cap nationwide.

## 5. No Payments for the First Five Years

For the first five years, you didn't have to pay anything towards the capital or the interest on the equity loan. It was essentially interest-free money for that initial period.

However, from year six onwards, interest payments kicked in, starting at 1.75% of the loan amount and increasing annually by RPI plus 1%. This is the stage where many Help to Buy borrowers need to think carefully about their options: remortgaging to pay off the equity loan, or continuing with the increasing interest charges.

## What Now?

If you bought through Help to Buy and you're approaching or past year five, now's the time to review your position. The interest charges from year six onwards can add up, and remortgaging to clear the equity loan could save you money in the long run.
