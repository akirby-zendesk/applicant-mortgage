When people start looking for a mortgage, the first instinct is usually to walk into their bank. It makes sense because you've been with them for years, they know your finances, and there's a branch down the road. But here's something most people don't realise: your bank adviser can only recommend your bank's own mortgages.

That's a problem, because there are over 90 active mortgage lenders in the UK. Your bank is just one of them.

## What Does "Whole of Market" Actually Mean?

In the UK, the Financial Conduct Authority requires every mortgage adviser to tell you the scope of their service before they make a recommendation. There are essentially three types:

- **Tied advisers** work for a single lender. A mortgage adviser in a Barclays branch can only offer Barclays products. They might have some decent rates, but if another lender has a better deal for your circumstances, they simply can't tell you about it.

- **Multi-tied advisers** have access to a panel of lenders, typically somewhere between 10 and 30. This is better, but it still means the majority of the market is invisible to them.

- **Whole-of-market advisers** search across the entire lending market. That means over 90 lenders and thousands of individual mortgage products. No restrictions, no panel limitations. Just the widest possible search to find what's right for you.

## The Deals You'd Never Find on Your Own

Here's something that surprises a lot of people: dozens of mortgage lenders in the UK don't deal directly with the public at all. They're broker-only, which means the only way to access their products is through an independent adviser.

These aren't obscure lenders either. They include some of the most competitive options on the market, particularly for people whose circumstances don't fit neatly into a high street bank's tick-box criteria. If you're self-employed, a contractor, working in the Construction Industry Scheme, or have a more complex income structure, broker-only lenders often have far more flexible underwriting than the big banks.

If you only speak to your bank, these options are completely invisible to you.

## It's Not Just About Rates

Finding a low interest rate is important, but it's not the whole picture. Different lenders have different approaches to:

- How they assess your income, since some lenders will annualise your gross income while others want two or three years of accounts
- How they treat overtime, commission, or bonus pay
- Their attitude to credit history, where a missed payment three years ago might rule you out with one lender and be completely fine with another
- Product fees, early repayment charges, and flexibility around overpayments
- How quickly they process applications, since some lenders are weeks faster than others

A whole-of-market broker doesn't just compare rates. We match your full circumstances, including income, deposit, credit history, and future plans, against the criteria of every lender available to find the one that genuinely fits.

## "But My Bank Will Look After Me Because I'm a Loyal Customer"

This is one of the most common assumptions we hear, and unfortunately it's rarely true. Banks price their mortgages to attract new customers. Loyalty doesn't typically translate into better rates or more flexible terms.

In fact, many people who stick with their existing bank end up paying more than they need to, because they never compared what else was out there. A whole-of-market search takes the guesswork out of it entirely.
