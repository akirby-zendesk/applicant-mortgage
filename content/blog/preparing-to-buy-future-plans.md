Welcome to the final day of my five-day guide to preparing to buy a new home. Over the past four days we've covered [knowing your deposit](/blog/preparing-to-buy-know-your-deposit), [checking your credit history](/blog/preparing-to-buy-credit-history), [gathering your income documents](/blog/preparing-to-buy-income-documents), and [understanding your outgoings](/blog/preparing-to-buy-outgoings).

Today's topic ties everything together: **be clear about your future plans.**

## Why Your Adviser Needs to Know

It's essential that your mortgage adviser is clear on your future plans so they can recommend the right mortgage for you. The product that's perfect for someone staying put for five years might be completely wrong for someone who plans to move again in two.

## The Questions to Think About

Be really clear with your adviser about what the next few years look like. For example:

**Are you planning structural changes?** Are you looking to buy the property and then extend or renovate in a couple of years? This could mean you need to borrow more in the future, and the right mortgage now should accommodate that.

**Is this a long-term home?** Maybe you do want to make changes to the property, but the reality is you're not going to consider it until your newborn has reached school age. That changes the calculation entirely.

**Are you planning to have children?** Going down to one income, even temporarily, has a significant impact on affordability. It's better to plan for that now than to find yourself stretched later. On the positive side, it might also mean avoiding childcare costs.

**Is this a stepping stone?** Maybe your next home is a stop-gap, and for the next couple of years all you want to do is make the absolute minimum mortgage payments while you save for the property you really want.

**Are you changing jobs?** If you've already got something lined up, have you got the offer in writing? Can you provide it to us? On the flip side, are you expecting a reduction in your income?

## The Key Point

At the end of the day, the last thing your mortgage adviser wants to do is recommend a product that you won't be able to afford in the future. The more honest and open you are about your plans, the better we can match you to the right deal.
