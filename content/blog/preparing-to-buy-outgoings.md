Welcome to day four of my five-day guide to preparing to buy a new home. Today we're talking about your outgoings and having clarity on the things you're paying for.

Your borrowing potential will be calculated based on your **affordability moving forward**, not just your income. So while it's important for your adviser to pay close attention to your existing monthly bill payments, you also need to consider how your costs might change with a new home.

## Think Ahead, Not Just Where You Are Now

If you're thinking of moving from a two-bed home to a four-bed home with a study, for example, several things will change:

- **Council tax band:** a larger property likely means a higher band
- **Running costs:** gas, electric, and water bills will all be affected
- **Buildings and contents insurance:** we can provide you with a quote for your new home

These might seem like small details, but they add up, and lenders take them into account.

## Travel Costs

Will your commute change? Try to work out your new travel costs as best you can, whether that's petrol for a longer drive or the price of a train season ticket from a different station. If your work travel costs are going to increase significantly, that affects what a lender sees as affordable.

## Protection: Know Where You Stand

It always surprises me just how many clients come to me and don't actually know how much death-in-service cover they receive from their employer, if they're fortunate enough to receive any at all.

More concerning is the client who has no emergency savings to draw upon, receives little or no sick pay, and yet doesn't have an income protection policy in place. Be sure of exactly where you stand if you become ill and unable to work.

For any **life, critical illness, or income protection policies** you do have in place, dig out the documents so your adviser can check their suitability against the new mortgage and loan term. This is important to do now because any change in your policy premiums could affect your affordability.

## Other Commitments to Consider

- **Car finance:** if you're paying for a car with an end date fast approaching, consider your plans. If you're going to replace that finance, will it be more expensive?
- **Childcare:** moving home can affect your childcare arrangements. Consider any increases in nursery, breakfast club, or after-school club costs.
- **General spending:** there's never a bad time to review your household expenditure. Maybe there are some changes you could make that would allow you to start building savings.

## The Takeaway

The clearer you are about your outgoings, both current and future, the more accurate your affordability picture will be. This means better advice, better lender matching, and fewer surprises during the application.
