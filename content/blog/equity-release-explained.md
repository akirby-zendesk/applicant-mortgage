Here's a quick question for the over-55s: have you ever gone to work and wondered how your colleague of the past 10 years has suddenly found a way to gift a significant sum to their children to help them buy a new home?

It could be that they've looked into their equity release options.

## What Is Equity Release?

Equity release allows homeowners aged 55 and over to access some of the value tied up in their property without having to sell it or move out. The money can be taken as a lump sum, in smaller amounts over time, or a combination of both.

For many people, it's a way to unlock wealth that's been sitting in bricks and mortar for decades, wealth that would otherwise only be accessible by downsizing or selling.

## It's Not for Everyone

Equity release is a significant financial decision, and it's not going to be the right option for every homeowner. That's exactly why my role as an equity release adviser is to make sure it's the right thing for you, and that we fully explore all the other alternatives that are out there first.

There are implications for inheritance, means-tested benefits, and the overall cost over time. These need to be understood clearly before any decision is made.

## What Are the Alternatives?

Before committing to equity release, we'd always look at whether there are other ways to achieve what you're looking for. That might include:

- **Remortgaging** to a standard residential product
- **Downsizing** to a smaller property and freeing up cash
- **A retirement interest-only mortgage** where you pay the interest monthly
- **Savings or other assets** that could meet the need without touching your property

Only once we've explored everything else would we look at whether equity release is the right fit.
