Welcome to day one of my five-day guide to preparing to buy a new home. Over the next five days, I'll be sharing some of the important steps I discuss with my clients when they first make contact.

First things first: **know your deposit.**

It sounds obvious, but it's absolutely key to the whole process of finding the right mortgage for you.

## Why Your Deposit Size Matters

Different lenders have different products, and we all know that. They all have their own lending criteria too. But what you might not have thought about is that those lending criteria can change depending on what your deposit is, even with the same bank.

For example, the most suitable lender for you might actually lend you *more*, if that's what you need, by putting together a better deposit. Moving from a 5% deposit to a 10% deposit doesn't just get you a better interest rate. It can change which products are available to you and how much you're able to borrow.

## How to Work Out Your Deposit

If you already own a property, you want to be looking at the equity within your home. To work this out, you'll need:

- **An up-to-date valuation** of your current home
- **Your latest mortgage balance:** check a recent statement or log into your lender's portal
- **Any early repayment charges:** you could have fees for ending your existing mortgage early, so it's always important to check. Your mortgage adviser will help you with this.

Then there are your **savings**, money you may already have put to one side for a rainy day or in preparation for your next move.

It's really important to remember that you probably don't want to use all the savings you've got, because there are going to be other costs too: stamp duty, solicitor's fees, removal costs, and so on.

## The Bank of Mum and Dad

Some of you may be fortunate enough to have access to another source of funds: a **gifted deposit** from a close family member. If this is a topic you've touched on before with your family, then maybe it's time to start having some more serious discussions about it. Knowing whether this is an option early on can make a real difference to what's available to you.
