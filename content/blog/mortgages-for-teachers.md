If there's one thing I've learned from helping teachers with their mortgages, it's this: you are some of the most time-constrained clients out there.

Between lesson planning, marking, parents' evenings, and everything else that comes with the job, finding time to sit down and talk about your mortgage can feel impossible during term time. That's exactly why the school holidays are your window of opportunity.

## Why the Holidays Matter

Whether you're looking to move home in the next few months, have a remortgage coming up, or you're thinking about getting into the buy-to-let market and don't really know where to start, the holidays are the optimal time to reach out and start having these conversations.

There's no rush, no squeezing a phone call into your lunch break, no trying to read through documents after a full day of teaching. You've got the time and headspace to actually focus on what could be one of the biggest financial decisions you'll make.

## Changing Schools? That's Not a Problem

Something I hear a lot from teachers is worry about changing employers. You know you're about to move to a different school, and you're concerned that might cause problems with a mortgage application.

Here's the good news: it's not necessarily an issue. There are lenders out there who will accept your new contract offer as part of the application process. So if you've got a new role lined up for September, that doesn't have to hold you back from getting things moving now.

## Get Your Documents Holiday-Ready

While you've got the time, it's worth making sure you have everything you'd need for a mortgage application:

- **Bank statements:** make sure you've got access to your accounts and recent statements
- **Proof of identity and address:** check these are still up to date and valid
- **Your new contract offer:** if you're about to change schools, have this to hand
- **Payslips:** your last three months, ideally

Getting these sorted now means that when we do sit down together, we can move quickly rather than waiting for paperwork to arrive.
